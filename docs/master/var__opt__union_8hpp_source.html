182  /*
183  IMPORTANT NOTE: the "gadget" in the union object appears to be a varopt sketch,
184  but in fact is NOT because it doesn't satisfy the mathematical definition
185  of a varopt sketch of the concatenated input streams. Therefore it could be different
186  from a true varopt sketch with that value of K, in which case it could easily provide
187  worse estimation accuracy for subset-sum queries.
188 
189  This should not surprise you; the approximation guarantees of varopt sketches
190  do not apply to things that merely resemble varopt sketches.
191 
192  However, even though the gadget is not a varopt sketch, the result
193  of the unioning process IS a varopt sketch. It is constructed by a
194  somewhat complicated "resolution" process which determines the largest K
195  that a valid varopt sketch could have given the available information,
196  then constructs a varopt sketch of that size and returns it.
197 
198  However, the gadget itself is not touched during the resolution process,
199  and additional sketches could subsequently be merged into the union,
200  at which point a varopt result could again be requested.
201  */
202 
203  /*
204  Explanation of "marked items" in the union's gadget:
205 
206  The boolean value "true" in an pair indicates that the item
207  came from an input sketch's R zone, so it is already the result of sampling.
208 
209  Therefore it must not wind up in the H zone of the final result, because
210  that would imply that the item is "exact".
211 
212  However, it is okay for a marked item to hang out in the gadget's H zone for a while.
213 
214  And once the item has moved to the gadget's R zone, the mark is never checked again,
215  so no effort is made to ensure that its value is preserved or even makes sense.
216  */
217 
218  /*
219  Note: if the computer could perform exact real-valued arithmetic, the union could finalize
220  its result by reducing k until inner_tau > outer_tau. [Due to the vagaries of floating point
221  arithmetic, we won't attempt to detect and specially handle the inner_tau = outer_tau special
222  case.]
223 
224  In fact, we won't even look at tau while while reducing k. Instead the logic will be based
225  on the more robust integer quantity num_marks_in_h_ in the gadget. It is conceivable that due
226  to round-off error we could end up with inner_tau slightly less than outer_tau, but that should
227  be fairly harmless since we will have achieved our goal of getting the marked items out of H.
228 
229  Also, you might be wondering why we are bothering to maintain the numerator and denominator
230  separately instead of just having a single variable outer_tau. This allows us (in certain
231  cases) to add an input's entire R-zone weight into the result sketch, as opposed to subdividing
232  it then adding it back up. That would be a source of numerical inaccuracy. And even
233  more importantly, this design choice allows us to exactly re-construct the input sketch
234  when there is only one of them.
235  */
